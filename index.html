A time line of Chris Hadfield's life


  • 1959 - Chris Hadfield was born in Sarnia, Ontario in Canada on the 29th of August 1959. As a young boy Chris watched Apollo 11 lang on the moon and he knew he wanted to become an astronaut the moment he saw the landing.

  • 1974 - Hadfield's compassion at this point was to fly planes and then become an astronaut so after hard work and a lot of desperation Chris won a scholarship to be able to be taught and get the license to fly gliders.

  • 1978 - This is the year Chris Hadfield joined the Canadian armed forces for an military education because back then that was a good path to take if you wanted to become an astronaut. He spent four years here and got a Bachelors Degree in Mechanical Engineering.

  • 1992 - In May 1992 Chris Hadfield was awarded with a Master's Degree in Aviation Systems from the University of Tennessee Space Institute, After many years of very hard work Chris Hadfield had finally been chosen from 5330 applicants that had applied to the Canadian Space Agency in June of 1992 he was destined to go into space.

  • 1995 - In November of 1995 Chris Hadfield became the first and only Canadian to ever board Mir, a Russian spaceship. He was tasked to serve as the mission specialist to deliver supplies to the Russians. They flew on the space shuttle Atlantis.

  • 2001 - Chris Hadfield in 2001 to 2003 was the newly appointed Commander of Operations for NASA in Russia's cosmonaut program, during this time Chris had his second space flight in which during the 11-day flight he performed two spacewalks and installed Canada arm 2.

  • 2012 - In 2012, Chris was assigned his last space flight, expedition 34/35, after dealing with scar tissue possibly not allowing him to go he left on the Russian Soyuz to get to the ISS, he also became the Commander of the ISS during the second half of his 5-month stay on the ISS. On May 13 he and his teammates landed in Kazakhstan after traveling about 99 million kilometers. As of July 3rd Chris Hadfield has retired from all space duties in light of a life spent more freely

Chris Hadfield’s Special Honors


Aside from being the first Canadian to walk in space, Chris is a heavily decorated astronaut, engineer, and pilot. Colonel Hadfield’s many awards include:


  1. Liethen-Tittle Award (1988): This award is given to the top pilot graduate of the USAF Test Pilot School. Chris was named the Top Test Pilot in both the US Air Force and the US Navy (1991).

  2. Order of Ontario (1996): The highest-honor award in Canada, the Order of Ontario is given to individuals who achieves a high level of excellence.

  3. The Meritorious Service Cross (2001 and 2013) Chris received medals for both the military (2001) and civilian (2013) categories of this medal, which is given to those who demonstrate meritorious acts.

  4. The NASA Exceptional Service Medal (2002): This medal for exceptional service is given to members of NASA who contribute to the progress and improvement of the organization.

  5. Golden Jubilee Medal (2003): This award was established by Queen Elizabeth II and is bestowed upon any nominee who demonstrates a large contribution to public life.

  6. Aviation Hall of Fame (2005): Chris was inducted into Canada’s Aviation Hall of Fame in 2005.

  7. Diamond Jubilee Medal (2012): The Diamond Jubilee Medal is given to exceptional Canadians who have contributed significantly to society.

  8. Shorty Award for Best in Science (2013): This award is given to individuals for unique and interesting content, and Chris won it in 2013 for his promotion of space and science with his videos.

  9. The Order of Canada (2014): Established by Queen Elizabeth II, this award is the second-highest honor for merit, bestowed upon those who demonstrate outstanding service to Canada.
